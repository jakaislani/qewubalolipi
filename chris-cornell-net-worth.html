Career

Chris Cornell’s career began when he was 16 years old and formed the band The Shemps with his high school friends Kim Thayil and Hiro Yamamoto.

Unfortunately, the band didn’t last very long and was torn apart just after a year.

Later in 1984, Cornell reunited with Thayil and Yamamoto to form the greatest grunge band of Soundgarden.

In 1990, Soundgarden’s debut album earned them a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance.

Moreover, four years later, he formed a band called Temple of The Dog and released a song titled Hunger Strike as a tribute to his late friend Andrew Wood.

In 1994, Chris announced the collapse of the band Soundgarden.

Then Cornell started to pursue his solo career and landed his debut album entitled Euphoria Morning in 1999.

In 2001, he alongside his two friends formed yet another legendary band named Audioslave.

Out of Exile, their second album, was a worldwide hit and made it to the top of the Billboard 200 chart.

In 2006 he split up with the band Audioslave and stated that it was due to some incorrigible difference among band members.

A grunge legend Cornell toured worldwide, including England, Germany, Brazil, Argentina, and another 25 nations.

In 2011 Cornell released an original song for the movie Machine Gun Preacher which was entitled The Keeper.

Likewise, He went on tour around North America and later continued his tour in 2012-2013 and performed in several venues in Europe and the U.S.

Sadly, He released his last single was The Promise which was released in 2017.

Chris Cornell will always be remembered as one of the best rock and grunge artists of all time.

Unfortunately, Chris Cornell passed away on May 18, 2017, in Detroit.

Further, the rockstar performed in a concert hours before he committed suicide in the bathroom of MGM Grand hotel.

Moreover, He had battled depression for his whole life and admitted to having had struggled with suicidal thoughts in the past.

In addition, the police ruled that his death was a suicide, but his widow questioned this conclusion. , as drugs prescribed were present in higher concentrations than what should have been prescribed.

Even after four years since he passed away, he and his music are still popular and prominent to many people.
