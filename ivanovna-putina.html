Vladimir Putin Career

Putin was a KGB foreign intelligence officer before becoming Prime Minister of Russia in 1999. He was elected to his first term as President less than four months later. Because he was legally restricted to two consecutive terms as President, Putin served as prime minister again under Dmitry Medvedev from 2008 to 2012. He returned to the President in 2012 in an election marked by claims of fraud and demonstrations; he reelected again in 2018. During his first term as President, the Russian economy grew for eight years in a row, with GDP measured by purchasing power increasing by 72%, real incomes increasing by a factor of 2.5, real wages more than tripling, unemployment and poverty more than halving, and Russians’ self-assessed life satisfaction increasing significantly.

Putin was instrumental in Russia’s triumph in the Second Chechen War and its victory in the Russo-Georgian War. During his third term as President, falling oil prices, combined with international sanctions, resulted in a 3.7 percent drop in GDP in 2015. The Russian economy recovered in 2016 with 0.3 percent GDP growth. Other achievements under Putin have included pipeline construction and the reinstatement of the GLONASS satellite navigation system.
