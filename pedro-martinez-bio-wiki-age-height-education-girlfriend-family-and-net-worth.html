    2019-2020

    Martínez made his Grand Slam debut in the main draw at the year 2019 French launch in singles and at the 2020 French launch in doubles with Christian Garin. He reached his first ATP quarterfinal in his career at the ATP 500 2020 Rio launched in Brazil defeating Hugo Dellien and Pablo Andujar. He made the third round of the year 2020 French launched in singles in June, his best Grand Slam result thus far. As a result, he entered the top 100 in singles at World number 97 on 12 October 2020.

    2021: French Open doubles semifinal & top 100, first ATP final, top 60 in singles

    Martínez reached the third round of the 2021 Australian launch in singles for the first time at this Major, defeating Emil Ruusuvuori, his second showing at this level in his career.
    At the 2021 French launch, he reached the semifinals as an alternate in doubles with fellow Spaniard Pablo Andujar, with whom he made his Grand Slam doubles debut as a pair at the year 2021 Australian Open. They defeated the fourteenth-seeded Belgians Sander Gillé/Joran Vliegen in the third round and the pair of Rohan Bopanna/Franko Skugor in the quarterfinals. They entered the tournament as a replacement alternate pair for the top seeds Nikola Mektić/Mate Pavić.On 14 June 2021, As a result, he made his top 100 debuts in doubles at world number 95 for the first time in his career. On his debut, he reached the third round of the 2021 Wimbledon Championships in singles for the first time in his career, defeating thirteenth seed Gael Monfils, his 3rd showing in the 3rd round of a Grand Slam.
    He reached his first ATP final at the year 2021 Generali launch Kitzbühel where he was defeated by Casper Ruud. He secured the biggest win of his career when he overcame world number sixteen Roberto Bautista Agut en route to the championship match. He also reached the semifinals at the same tournament partnering with Marc Polmans. As a result, he reached a new career-high singles ranking of world number 76 and a doubles ranking of number 88 on 2 August 2021. At the year 2021 US launch he recorded his first win at this Major over James Duckworth to reach the second round. After winning the Challenger in Seville, he reached a career-high ranking of world number fifty-nine on 13 September 2021.
    At the year 2021 Kremlin Cup he was defeated by Marin Cilic in only the second quarterfinal of the season and 3rd in his career at the ATP level. He reached a career-high singles ranking of number fifty-eight on 25 October 2021. At the same tournament, he also reached the semifinals partnering with Ilya Ivashka. As a result, he returned to a doubles ranking of number eight seven on 25 October 2021 tied with his best ranking thus far.

    2022: ATP Cup finalist, first ATP singles & doubles titles, Top 40

    Martínez participated for the first time in the year 2022 ATP Cup as part of the Spanish team where he played doubles with Alejandro Davidovich Fokina and Albert Ramos Vinolas lost both matches and helped Spain reach the final.
    He reached the 3rd round of 2022 Australian launched in doubles for the first time at this Major partnering compatriot Pablo Andujar. As a result, he reached a new career-high doubles ranking of World number seventy-two on 31 January 2022.
    He was seeded fourth in Santiago. After receiving a first-round bye, he defeated Jaume Munar, Yannick Hanfmann and Alejandro Tabilo to reach his second ATP final. He beat Sebastián Báez in the final to win his first ATP title. As a result, he made his top fifty debuts on 28 February 2022.
    He reached the 3rd round of a Masters 1000 for the first time in his career at the 2022 Miami launch. He made his top forty debuts following the 2022 Mutua Madrid launch on 9 May 2022.
    At the 2022 Rakuten Japan launches Tennis Championships he reached the quarterfinals for a second time at an ATP 500 tour level, defeating Alexei Popyrin and Jaume Munar.
